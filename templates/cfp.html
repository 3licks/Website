Call for Participation

Electromagnetic Field is a UK camping festival for those with an inquisitive mind or an interest in making things: hackers, artists, geeks, crafters, scientists, and engineers. It's happening near Bletchley, Buckinghamshire on August 29th — 31st, and tickets are on sale now for about Â£95. Our last festival in 2012 had 600 attendees; this year we'll have over 1,200.

We're looking for people to talk, share, and demonstrate the things they love. We're especially keen to hear from people with different backgrounds than you'd expect find at a technology conference. We're not just looking for talks: we also want installations, workshops, and games.

At previous events we've heard about everything from genetic modification to electronics, blacksmithing to high-energy physics, reverse engineering to lock picking, computer security to crocheting, and quadcopters to beer brewing.

If you're enthusiastic about it, we are too! You don't need to be a professional or an expert to present: impostors are welcome.

Selection

After you submit a proposal, it will be considered anonymously by a diverse selection panel. We may take into account other factors later on in the process to see where we can support you.

EMF is a non-profit event run by unpaid volunteers. All of the ticket price goes towards making the festival a great place to be for a weekend, and everyone who attends contributes to make it a success.

Because of this, we'd like everyone who participates to buy a ticket, and we don't have the budget to pay speakers. We have a limited amount of money set aside to help those who otherwise wouldn't be able to afford to come, or who can only attend for the day of their talk. Please let us know if that's the case.

Submit a proposal

We need a rough idea of what you want to present at EMF, what sort of resources you'll need, and how long you'll expect it to last. If you need some help writing your proposal, we're happy to help. If you're not confident talking we'll help you out however we can.
