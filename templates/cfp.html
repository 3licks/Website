If you'd like to give a talk or workshop at EMF, we need some simple information from you.

Electromagnetic Field is a UK camping festival for those with an inquisitive mind or an interest in making things: hackers, artists, geeks, crafters, scientists, and engineers. It's happening in Milton Keynes on August 29th - 31st, and tickets are on sale now for about 95 GBP. In 2012 we had over 500 people, this year we'll have over 1,250. We're looking for people to talk, share, and demonstrate the things they love. We're especially interested to hear from people from different backgrounds, races, genders, and ages. We've had talks on everything from computer security to crocheting, genetic modification to electronics, blacksmithing to high-energy physics, reverse engineering to lock picking, quadcopters to beer brewing, bees to the space shuttle, scientific cooking to zombie makeup, and everything in between. We want to show things people can make, explore, and play with, rather than things people can buy, consume, or subscribe to. If you're enthusiastic about it, we are too! You don't need to be a professional or an expert to present: Impostors are welcome. We're looking for talks about 30 minutes long, but we can give your more or less time if you want. We also want art installations, workshops, and games too. When you submit a proposal a diverse panel will select talks based on your proposal without knowing who you are. We may take into account other factors later on in the process to see where we can support you. We have some money set aside to help those who wouldn't be able to afford coming: Get in touch if you need it. We need a rough idea of what you intend to present at EMF, what sort of resources, props, or equipment you'll need, and how long you'll expect it to last. If you need some help writing your proposal, Ask. If you're not confident talking we'll provide training and support before your slot. From you we'll need the following things: A one sentence summary (it should fit in a tweet): Longer, but not too rambling explanation: What format: talk | workshop | installation | other How long do you need: 5 | 15 | 30 | 45 | A few hours | One day | Two days | All weekend Have you given a talk/workshop before: It's my first time! | I've talked before | I've given this talk before What sort of equipment will you need to run this? Will the audience require anything in advance? If you're comfortable telling us a little bit about yourself, we'd appreciate it. We won't be using this to select talks but we want to know how well we're doing with outreach.
